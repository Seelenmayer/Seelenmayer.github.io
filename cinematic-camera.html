    Cinematic Camera

    This project showcases my cinematic camera system in Unity, inspired by platformers like Klonoa. The player, camera, and look-at target each follow separate splines, ensuring smooth movement and framing.

    The camera follows an interval system: the player pushes the upper bound forward while the lower bound follows at a set distance. The camera moves only when the lower bound reaches it, smoothly interpolating along its spline while continuously adjusting its look-at rotation.

    Obstructing objects near the camera are burned away using a GLSL shader. A noise texture, generated in GIMP, guides the burn effect, which reverses when the camera moves away.

    camera.mp4

    Initially, the shader mapped screen space coordinates using color channels—red for X-axis, green for Y-axis. This ensured correct positioning before overlaying the noise texture in blue.

    shader1.png
    shader2.png

    A premultiplied alpha issue caused hard edges in the burn effect. Premultiplied alpha precomputes color values with transparency for faster rendering.

    shader3.png

    The final shader lacked shadow casting, requiring integration with Unity’s HDRP "Lit" shader. This involved resolving namespace conflicts and selecting the correct render pass. I opted for forward rendering to maintain transparency without overhauling the pipeline.
